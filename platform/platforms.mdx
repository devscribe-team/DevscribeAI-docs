## Overview

Each platform is a separate documentation project with its own content, website, and team members. You can create multiple platforms, invite collaborators, and assign specific access permissions.

## Creating a New Platform

<Steps>
<Step title="Access platform creation">
Navigate to **Settings** > **Organization** and click the **Create Platform** button in the Platforms section.
</Step>

<Step title="Name your platform">
Enter a descriptive name for your platform. This name will be used for:
- The platform's documentation website URL
- The GitHub/GitLab repository name
- Display throughout the interface

<Callout type="tip">
Choose a clear, lowercase name without special characters (e.g., "api-docs", "developer-guide").
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step title="Platform provisioning">
After clicking **Create**, the system automatically:
1. Creates the platform in your organization
2. Adds you as a platform member
3. Provisions a GitHub or GitLab repository (based on your org settings)
4. Sets up Vercel deployment infrastructure
5. Generates your platform's deployment URL

<Callout type="info">
Platform creation typically takes 30-60 seconds. You'll be notified once deployment is complete.
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step title="Verify creation">
Your new platform appears in the Platforms list showing:
- Platform name
- Number of members
- Deployment status

<Callout type="success">
You can now access your platform's repository and begin adding documentation content.
</Callout>
</Step>
</Steps>

## Inviting Team Members

### Sending Invitations

<Steps>
<Step title="Open invitation modal">
In **Settings** > **Organization**, click **Invite Teammate** in the Invitations section.
</Step>

<Step title="Configure invitation details">
Fill in the following fields:

**Email Address** (required)
- Enter the email address of the person you're inviting
- Must be a valid email format

**Platform Access** (optional)
- Select a specific platform from the dropdown, or
- Leave blank to invite them to the organization without platform access

**Role** (required)
- **Member**: Standard access to assigned platforms
- **Admin**: Full organization access including platform management, member management, and settings

<Callout type="warning">
Admin role grants organization-wide permissions. Use member role for contributors who only need access to specific platforms.
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step title="Send invitation">
Click **Invite** to send the invitation email. The system will:
1. Create a pending invitation with a unique token
2. Send an email with an invitation link to the recipient
3. Set a 14-day expiration on the invitation

<Callout type="info">
Recipients have 14 days to accept the invitation before it expires. You can resend expired invitations.
</Callout>
</Step>
</Steps>

### Managing Pending Invitations

View all pending invitations in the Invitations section. Each invitation displays:
- Email address
- Assigned platform (if any)
- Current status (pending/accepted)
- Role assignment
- Action menu

You can:
- **Update** invitation details before acceptance
- **Delete** unwanted invitations
- **Search** invitations by email address

<Callout type="tip">
If you invited someone to the wrong platform, update the invitation before they accept it rather than removing and re-adding them later.
</Callout>

## Understanding Roles

### Member Role

Members have access to assigned platforms only. They can:
- View and edit documentation content
- Access platform repositories
- View platform deployment settings

Members cannot:
- Create or delete platforms
- Invite other team members
- Access organization settings
- Manage roles

### Admin Role

Admins have full organization access. They can:
- Create and delete platforms
- Invite and remove team members
- Change member roles
- Assign members to platforms
- Access all organization settings
- View and manage all platforms

<Callout type="warning">
Admins automatically have access to all platforms in the organization, regardless of specific platform assignments.
</Callout>

## Managing Platform Access

### Adding Members to a Platform

<Steps>
<Step title="Open platform members">
Click on a platform card in the Platforms section, then select **View Members** from the dropdown menu.
</Step>

<Step title="Add member">
Click **Add Member** in the platform members modal.
</Step>

<Step title="Select member">
The member list shows organization members who aren't already on this platform. You can:
- Search by name using the search bar
- Select a member from the filtered list
- Click **Add** to grant platform access

<Callout type="success">
The member immediately gains access to the platform's repository and documentation.
</Callout>
</Step>
</Steps>

### Removing Members from a Platform

<Steps>
<Step title="Access platform members">
Open the platform's member list from the Platforms section.
</Step>

<Step title="Remove member">
Click the dropdown menu next to a member's name and select **Remove**.
</Step>

<Step title="Confirm removal">
Confirm the action. The member loses access to this platform but remains in your organization.

<Callout type="info">
Removing a member from a platform doesn't delete them from your organization. They retain access to other assigned platforms.
</Callout>
</Step>
</Steps>

### Bulk Platform Assignment

Assign members to multiple platforms simultaneously:

<Steps>
<Step title="Open member actions">
In the Organization Members section, click the dropdown menu next to a member's name and select **Choose Platforms**.
</Step>

<Step title="Select platforms">
Check the platforms you want this member to access. Previously assigned platforms are pre-selected.
</Step>

<Step title="Apply changes">
Click **Assign**. The system will:
- Add the member to newly selected platforms
- Remove the member from unselected platforms

<Callout type="danger">
If you deselect all platforms, the member will lose access to all platforms. You'll be asked to confirm this action.
</Callout>
</Step>
</Steps>

## Changing Member Roles

<Steps>
<Step title="Access role management">
In the Organization Members section, locate the member and click their dropdown menu. Select **Change Role**.
</Step>

<Step title="Select new role">
Choose either:
- **Member**: Restricts access to assigned platforms only
- **Admin**: Grants full organization access

<Callout type="warning">
Changing a member from admin to member doesn't affect their platform assignments, but they lose organization management capabilities.
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step title="Confirm change">
Click **Change Role**. The role update takes effect immediately across the organization.

<Callout type="success">
The member's new role is reflected throughout the platform with updated permissions.
</Callout>
</Step>
</Steps>

## Removing Members from Organization

<Steps>
<Step title="Open member menu">
In the Organization Members section, click the dropdown menu next to the member's name and select **Delete from Organization**.
</Step>

<Step title="Confirm deletion">
Confirm the removal. This action:
- Removes the member from all platforms
- Deletes their organization access
- Cannot be undone

<Callout type="danger">
Deleting a member from the organization removes them from all platforms. To remove access to specific platforms only, use platform-level removal instead.
</Callout>
</Step>
</Steps>

## Search and Filter

All management sections include search functionality:

**Platforms**: Search by platform name to quickly locate specific documentation projects

**Invitations**: Filter by email address to find pending or accepted invitations

**Members**: Search by name to find organization members

<Callout type="tip">
Use search when managing large teams or multiple platforms to reduce visual clutter and find items faster.
</Callout>

## Common Workflows

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="Onboarding a new team member">
1. Click **Invite Teammate**
2. Enter their email address
3. Select **Member** role
4. Assign them to a specific platform
5. Click **Invite**

They'll receive an email with access instructions and have 14 days to accept.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Setting up a new documentation project">
1. Click **Create Platform**
2. Enter the project name
3. Wait for provisioning to complete
4. Invite team members or assign existing members
5. Share the repository URL with your team
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Promoting a member to admin">
1. Navigate to Organization Members
2. Find the member and open their dropdown menu
3. Select **Change Role**
4. Choose **Admin**
5. Confirm the change

Admins gain immediate access to all platforms and organization settings.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Reorganizing team access">
1. Locate the member in Organization Members
2. Select **Choose Platforms** from their dropdown
3. Check or uncheck platforms as needed
4. Click **Assign**

The system automatically adds or removes platform access based on your selections.
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="Invitation email not received">
- Verify the email address is correct in the Invitations list
- Check the recipient's spam/junk folder
- Ensure invitations haven't expired (14-day limit)
- Delete and resend the invitation if needed
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Cannot remove last admin">
Organizations require at least one admin. To remove the last admin:
1. Promote another member to admin first
2. Then change the original admin's role to member
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Member can't access platform">
Verify:
- The member is listed in the platform's member list
- Their organization role (admins have access to all platforms)
- They've accepted their organization invitation
- The platform deployment is active
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Platform creation failed">
Platform creation may fail if:
- The platform name is already in use
- GitHub/GitLab rate limits are exceeded
- Vercel deployment errors occur

Check your organization's integration status and try again. Contact support if the issue persists.
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
